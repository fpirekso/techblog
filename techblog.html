Interwiew

By Fenty

I interviewd my cousin Annisa because she grew up in the early 90s and life and technology was completely different than it was today.

Fenty: How old were you when you got your first computer?

Annisa: I've had a computer when I was in elemtary school I believe. I remember it being pretty blocky and huge, especially the screen.

Fenty: What was your favorite piece of technology when they were growing up?

Annisa: My favorite piece of technology growing up were the phones I've used, as time went on, it just kept evolving and able to do more stuff

Fenty: How has technology changed over your lifetime?

Annisa: Technology has changed over my lifetime in that what used to need a lot of space to save so little, it's the opposite. Now, you can save so much in a small phone. It also used to be that you needed different pieces of technology for different uses, but now you can find everything you need on a small phone.

Annisa: I believe the changes that I've seen in technology have very much helped society. Nowadays, you're able to grasp all the knowledge of the world in the palm of your hands. Fenty: What is your greatest hope for technology in the future?

Annisa: My greatest hope for technology is for it to be able to work with the environment. I hope to see that we continue evolving technology so it would save and protect the environment while continuing to help society progress with it's ability to access any information needed.

Fenty: What is your greatest fear for technology in the future?

Annisa: My greatest fear for technology is that people will only focus on advancing technology and profiting from said technology that they would forget to find ways to help better the environment and the lives of the unfortunate.

Fenty: What advice would you like to share with the younger generation about technology?

Annisa: The advice I would give to the younger generation would be, even though technology is greatly accessible to the majority, you also still need to learn how to live without it. Just take breaks sometimes and get involved in what's happening around you.
